It might sound crazy but coding without an internet connection can be insightful. 

"Imagine a box, and when you open it, all your friends are inside," said a friend. How can such a box be resisted? 

I find that even the inevitable digressions I take when researching a problem are more productive than the k-holes I find myself in online.

The source for this website alone includes some 21,882 javascript files and 4,572 markdown files. 

Having the time (not on deadlines) and the mental space (not online) to explore these files ...

For brushing up on old skills or learning new ones, the biggest hurdle to working offline is finding what you need.
